Software licences

What is a software licence?

A licence is essentially an agreement between the developer and the user.

What does the software licence include?

  • Permission to use the software
  • Usage rights
  • Number of users eligible to use the software
  • Inclusions with the software (such as support, maintenance, software upgrades)
  • Warranty terms
  • Permissions and limitations regarding the distribution of the software
  • Copyright definition
  • Terms for termination of the licence
  • Software performance guarantees
  • Penalties and fees for non-compliance

Licence types:

  • Free and open-source
  • Proprietary

Free and open-source:

  • Public domain licence
  • FOSS permissive licence
  • Copyleft

How licence agreements protect developers

  • They protect developers' intellectual property and trade secrets based on copyright laws.
  • They limit what other parties can do with the covered software code.
  • They limit the liability of the vendor.

How licence agreements protect users

  • They define what users can do with software code they did not write.
  • They establish how users stay in compliance with software licences, protect themselves from infringement claims and limit their legal liability.
  • They help users maintain a positive relationship with software developers and vendors.
  • They prevent overspending on licences by establishing clear parameters of how many licences an organisation needs.
